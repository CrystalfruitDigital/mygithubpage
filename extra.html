What I Play


Guitar:
I have been playing guitar on and off since 2008 when I bought my first electric guitar, a black Fender Squire Stratocaster with a pearl jam pick-guard. I took lessons at George's Music for 2 years and was one of the two guitarists of Tredyffrin-Eastown Middle School's jazz band. After being a part of the school band I found the structure and organization wasn't for me or my style of guitar playing. Instead, I would spend my time as a member of guitar jam sessions with close friends and learned that an improvised, messier style of jazz and blues was what I enjoyed playing the most. Even though I own two electric guitars and two acoustic guitars now, I still mainly use my first electric Stratocaster. I just feel at home when playing the guitar.

DANCE


I started break-dancing in late 2012


It all started when I saw my younger brother, my cousin and their friends break-dancing when I was passing by the track field. I was instantly intrigued. After connecting with them we found others who shared our interest in dance and soon I, along with David Jeong and James Kwak, co-founded Conestoga High School's Hip Hop Club.






Summer-Fall 2012

While the paperwork for the club is under way, we schedule meetings and practices at Wilson Farm Park.


Winter-Spring 2013

The club has been approved and we get to perform at the high school multi-cultural festival!

2 Years Later, Spring 2015

Over two years have passed and I still go back from time to time to visit the club. It has grown tremendously over such a short period of time (from around 12-15 to 40+ members!) and has steadily gained fame in Conestoga.

HACKATHONS


I saved the best for last


Although I've only been to two hackathons, I'm addicted. Gathering into teams, collaborating, learning, then going to code and design for 24-48 hours - everything about that just makes me say "yes!"

They're free (that includes the food!) and you get hands-on experience and swag in the form of t-shirts, sweaters and more thanks to the sponsors. All in all, hackathons are fantastic events that I would recommend to any designer or programmer to attend. It's worth it.


Google's Hack for Humanity


On December 12th-14th, 70 programmers and designers gathered at Google's headquarters in New York City, New York aspiring to find solutions to challenges both in society and abroad.
